### 🕒 Why Daily Planning Matters for JEE Aspirants

JEE covers a vast syllabus: Physics, Chemistry, and Math — each demanding intense conceptual clarity and regular practice.

A well-crafted daily schedule:

*   Eliminates decision fatigue
*   Ensures subject-wise balance
*   Builds consistency and long-term focus
*   Reduces burnout with built-in breaks

### 🗓️ Ideal Daily Routine for JEE 2025 Preparation (12–14 Study Hours)

| Time               | Task                                                    |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5:30 AM – 6:00 AM  | Wake up, freshen up                                     |
| 6:00 AM – 6:30 AM  | Meditation or light exercise                            |
| 6:30 AM – 9:00 AM  | **Study Block 1** – Physics (Numericals + Concepts)     |
| 9:00 AM – 9:30 AM  | Breakfast break                                         |
| 9:30 AM – 12:00 PM | **Study Block 2** – Math (New topics + NCERT Examples)  |
| 12:00 PM – 1:00 PM | Lunch + rest                                            |
| 1:00 PM – 3:00 PM  | **Study Block 3** – Chemistry (Organic + Physical)      |
| 3:00 PM – 3:30 PM  | Walk/stretch break (no screens!)                        |
| 3:30 PM – 5:30 PM  | **Study Block 4** – Mock test / PYQ analysis            |
| 5:30 PM – 6:30 PM  | Break / chat with family / tea                          |
| 6:30 PM – 8:00 PM  | **Study Block 5** – Weak topic revision / doubt-solving |
| 8:00 PM – 9:00 PM  | Dinner + unwind                                         |
| 9:00 PM – 10:00 PM | Light revision or topper strategy videos                |
| 10:00 PM           | Sleep (get your 7–8 hrs!) 😴                            |

---

### 🧩 Tips from Actual Toppers

*   **Plan weekly, review daily**: Use Sundays to outline weekly goals.
*   **One tough + one light subject per day**: Avoid burnout.
*   **Track Pomodoros**: 25–5-minute cycles boost focus.
*   **Use handwritten notes + NCERT as base**: Especially for Chemistry.
*   **Analyze mock tests, don’t just give them**: Learn from your mistakes.
