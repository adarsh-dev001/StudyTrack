Once you understand your current habits, you can implement proven techniques to manage your schedule more effectively.

### ğŸ… The Pomodoro Technique
This popular technique involves breaking your work into focused 25-minute intervals (called "Pomodoros") separated by short 5-minute breaks. After four Pomodoros, take a longer break of 15-30 minutes.

<Info title="How the Pomodoro Technique Helps">
- **Combats Procrastination:** The 25-minute commitment feels less daunting.
- **Improves Focus:** Short, timed intervals encourage deep work.
- **Reduces Burnout:** Regular breaks prevent mental fatigue.
- **Tracks Effort:** You can measure progress by the number of Pomodoros completed.
</Info>

## ğŸš« Section 4: Overcoming Procrastination

Procrastination is the thief of time and a major hurdle for many students.

<Warning title="The Vicious Cycle of Procrastination">
Delaying tasks leads to last-minute cramming, increased stress, lower quality work, and ultimately, poorer results. Recognizing the triggers for your procrastination is the first step to overcoming it.
</Warning>

**Strategies to Combat Procrastination:**
- **The 5-Minute Rule:** Commit to working on a task for just 5 minutes. Often, starting is the hardest part.
- **Reward Yourself:** Set up a system of rewards for completing tasks or study blocks.
- **Identify Your "Why":** Remind yourself of your long-term goals and why the task is important.
- **Tackle Difficult Tasks First:** Address your most challenging tasks when your energy levels are highest (often in the morning).
